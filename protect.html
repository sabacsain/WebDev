12 WAYS TO PROTECT YOURSELF FROM ONLINE FRAUD

1

Learn more about different kinds of internet fraud because the more you know about them
the better you can protect yourself from them.

2

Use common sense, if something sounds too good to be true or someone is offering a large amount of money in exchange for helping them in transferring funds out of a country, it’s probably a fraud.

3

Always keep your personal information secure and don’t leak your personal information to anyone, especially information about your savings, credits, checking, and other financial accounts.

4

Learn more about different kinds of internet fraud because the more you know about them the better you can protect yourself from them.

5

Guard your social security number especially carefully, as it is the most sought thing of many fraudsters. Leaking the number may result in damage that can take a long time to repair so don’t give this out except if it is necessary and if you are sure that you’re giving it to a legitimate and reputable company or individual.

6

Make deals or buy things only with companies and individuals that are legitimate and reputable. Investigate the background of the other party and try to search them on the web and find out if they are already widely trusted by different companies and individuals.

7

Buy directly from licensed shops or companies as cheaply priced items are often counterfeits.

8

Investigate the other parties’ contact details such as a physical office or shop addresses and phone numbers and make sure that they exist while also carefully verifying their credibility.

9

Be aware of the pitfalls of foreign commerce as it can be very difficult to deal with individuals or companies in foreign countries regarding warranties, returns, and laws concerning sales, so try to educate yourself on an overseas transaction process and its potential to be a fraud.

10

Understand carefully how the online sale or purchase process. Knowing how and where payments are made when the delivery is made, additional costs involved in the transactions and return, and refund policies can prevent you from getting involved in fraud.

11

Take your time in making transactions and avoid individuals and companies that pressure you to complete a transaction immediately as it is a common sign of fraud.

12

When buying using a credit card make sure that the other party is reputable and secure, make sure that the site’s URL starts with https, there is a locked padlock icon in the address bar, investigate the site’s TLS or SSL certificate issuer if it is both valid and up to date, use an updated version of your browser, and lastly use escrow service if needed.
