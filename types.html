10 TYPES OF ONLINE FRAUD

SPAM

Spam usually is a term used to describe electronic junk mail or messages sent to an email or mobile phone. This kind of message has a variety of contents and comes in large numbers. The purpose of these messages is to persuade you to purchase a product or service, visit a website where you can make purchases, or trick you into giving your information about your bank or credit card details.

SCAMS

With the use of the internet exchange of messages and information is now easy which also made scams flourish. Commonly cited scams are related to well-documented Nigerian Scams or Lotto Scams that use one form or another.

IDENTITY THEFT

Identity theft is a large part of online crimes where the theft and use of personal identifying information of an actual person, instead of using one’s fictitious identity. This can involve the use of personal information of people that could be dead or alive.

INTERNET BANKING FRAUD

Internet banking fraud is a theft or fraud done using online technology to illegally remove money from one bank account to another. It is usually a fraud in a form of identity theft and is made possible through different techniques such as phishing.

MALWARE

Generally malware is known as malicious software that damages or disables the users’ devices or steals personal and classified information.

5

6

RANSOMWARE

Ransomware is a type of online fraud where malware prevents users from accessing data and then demanding payment on the premise of restoring access and is usually sent through phishing techniques.

DENIAL OF SERVICE

Denial of service does what it says, it interrupts the user’s access to traffic to an online service, system, or network to cause harm and malicious intent to the user.

8

BUSINESS EMAIL COMPRMISE

It is an advanced and sophisticated form of fraud where it targets businesses that typically make wire payments. It accommodates legitimate email accounts with the use of social engineering techniques and then submits unauthorized payments.

PHISHING

Phishing is a popular technique used to gain personal information for identity theft. Phishing and other kinds of online fraud such as spam to fraudulently gain access to people’s online banking details. Phishing primarily targets online banking customers, online auction sites, or other online payment facilities. Usually, phishing emails will ask for the customer to follow a link to update personal bank account details and if followed leads the customer in downloading a program that collects his or her banking login details and sends them to a third party.
